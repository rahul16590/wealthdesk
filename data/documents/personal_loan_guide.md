# BNB Personal Loan Guide

## What is a Personal Loan

A personal loan is an unsecured loan -- you do not need to pledge any asset as collateral. Because the bank takes on more risk than with a secured loan, the interest rate is higher than a home loan or car loan. The funds are deposited directly into your account and you can use them for any purpose: medical expenses, a wedding, travel, education fees, or home renovation.

## Who is Eligible

- Indian resident, 21 to 58 years of age
- Salaried employees of government bodies, public sector undertakings, reputed private companies, or MNCs
- Minimum net monthly salary varies by city tier (metropolitan, urban, semi-urban); contact any BNB branch or call customer care for the current threshold applicable to your city
- Employment with the current employer for at least 1 year
- Credit score of 720 or above (CIBIL or equivalent)

Self-employed professionals (doctors, CAs, architects) are also eligible with 2 years of practice continuity and ITR proof.

## Loan Amount and Tenure

Personal loans at BNB are available in amounts from Rs. 50,000 up to Rs. 25 lakhs. The actual amount sanctioned depends on your income, credit profile, and existing loan obligations.

Tenure ranges from 12 months to 60 months (1 to 5 years). A shorter tenure means higher EMI and lower total interest paid. A longer tenure reduces the monthly burden but increases the total interest outgo.

## Required Documents

**Salaried applicants:**
- PAN card and Aadhaar card
- Last 3 months' salary slips
- Last 3 months' bank statement (salary account)
- Latest Form 16 or ITR

**Self-employed applicants:**
- PAN card and Aadhaar card
- Last 2 years' ITR with computation
- Last 6 months' bank statement (business account)
- Professional degree certificate (doctors, CAs, etc.)

## Processing Timeline

After document submission, BNB typically completes the credit assessment within 3 to 5 working days. Pre-approved offers for existing BNB customers with a good credit history may be processed within 24 hours.

## Processing Fee

A processing fee is charged as a percentage of the loan amount. This is deducted from the disbursed amount. Ask your Relationship Manager for the current fee applicable to your loan amount.

## Prepayment

Prepayment is permitted after 12 EMIs have been paid. A prepayment charge applies -- typically a percentage of the outstanding principal at the time of payment (the exact rate is in your loan agreement). Full foreclosure is possible at any time after 12 months, subject to the same charge.

Note: unlike floating-rate home loans (which carry no prepayment penalty under RBI guidelines), personal loans are fixed-rate and may attract a prepayment charge throughout the loan tenure.

## What the EMI Covers

Every EMI payment is split between principal repayment and interest. In the early months, a larger share goes toward interest. As the loan progresses, the principal repayment share increases. This is a standard amortisation schedule. The exact breakdown for each month is available in your loan statement on the BNB mobile app.

## If You Cannot Pay an EMI

One missed EMI will attract a late payment fee and a reminder call. Multiple missed payments will affect your CIBIL score, which makes future borrowing more expensive. If you are facing a genuine hardship, call the BNB customer care number listed in your loan agreement immediately. A restructuring facility is available for customers with a valid hardship.

## Common Use Cases

**Home renovation:** Personal loans for home improvement are popular because they are faster to process than top-up home loans and do not require documentation about the property.

**Medical emergency:** BNB offers an expedited processing track for medical emergency personal loans. Ask any branch about this facility.

**Education:** For education expenses not covered by an education loan (coaching fees, certifications, study materials), a personal loan is a common option. For degree programmes, an education loan with lower interest rates may be more suitable.

**Balance transfer:** If you have a personal loan with another bank at a higher rate, you can apply for a BNB balance transfer. Subject to credit assessment, BNB may offer a lower rate for the outstanding amount.
