# BNB Fixed Deposit Guide

## What is a Fixed Deposit

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a savings product where you deposit a lump sum with the bank for a fixed period. The bank pays you interest at a predetermined rate for the chosen tenure. At maturity, you receive your principal plus the accumulated interest. The interest rate is locked in at the time of booking, so market fluctuations after that do not affect your return.

FDs are one of the most predictable savings instruments available in India. They are insured up to Rs. 5 lakhs per depositor per bank under the Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC) scheme.

## Tenure Options

BNB offers FDs from 3 months up to 5 years. The available tenure bands are:

- Short term: 3 months, 6 months
- Medium term: 1 year, 2 years
- Long term: 5 years

Generally, longer tenures attract higher interest rates, though the specific rate for each tenure is reviewed periodically by the bank. For the current rate applicable to a specific tenure, please ask WealthDesk or visit any BNB branch.

## Minimum Deposit

The minimum deposit amount for a BNB FD is Rs. 10,000. There is no upper limit.

## Interest Payout Options

You can choose how you want to receive the interest:

**Cumulative (Compound) FD:** Interest is compounded quarterly and paid at maturity along with the principal. This maximises your return and is suitable for long-term goals.

**Non-Cumulative FD:** Interest is paid at regular intervals -- monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or annually. This is suitable for retirees or others who need a regular income stream.

## Premature Withdrawal

You can close an FD before maturity. The interest paid in that case is the rate applicable for the period the deposit was actually held, minus a premature withdrawal penalty. This means you may earn less than the contracted rate. The exact penalty applicable to your FD is shown in your FD advice document at the time of booking.

No premature withdrawal penalty applies in the case of the depositor's death. The nominee or legal heir can close the FD and receive the full interest accrued up to the date of closure.

## Loan Against FD

BNB offers a loan or overdraft facility against an FD, up to 90% of the FD amount. This lets you access funds in an emergency without breaking the FD and losing the contracted interest rate.

## Tax Saving FD (5-Year)

BNB's 5-year Tax Saving FD qualifies for a deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, up to Rs. 1.5 lakhs per financial year. Note that tax-saving FDs cannot be broken before 5 years and no loan can be taken against them.

## Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)

Interest earned on FDs is subject to TDS under Section 194A of the Income Tax Act. TDS is deducted when the total interest across all BNB FDs in a financial year exceeds the threshold set by the Income Tax Department. If your total income is below the taxable limit, submit Form 15G (or 15H for senior citizens) at the beginning of each financial year to avoid TDS deduction.

## Senior Citizen FDs

BNB offers a higher interest rate to depositors aged 60 years and above. Senior citizens earn an additional margin on top of the standard FD rate for all tenures. This additional benefit applies only when the primary account holder's age is 60 or above at the time of booking. Ask WealthDesk or visit any BNB branch for the current additional margin -- it is reviewed periodically alongside the standard FD rates.

## Nomination

Every FD must have a registered nominee. Without a nominee, in the event of the depositor's death, the funds go through a legal heirship process, which can take months. Register or update your nominee through the BNB mobile app or by visiting a branch.

## Auto-Renewal

You can instruct BNB to auto-renew the FD at maturity. The renewal happens at the rate prevailing on the maturity date, not the original rate. Review the renewal terms before setting this option.
